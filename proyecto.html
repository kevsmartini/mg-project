Briefing

01 / OBJECTIVE

SHADOW MOSES is an independent, non-profit fan-made project that reimagines, as a short film, Solid Snake's infiltration of the remote nuclear base of Shadow Moses, in the Fox archipelago of Alaska. It is not an official product: it is a love letter from the generation that grew up with the game, a tribute to the work of Hideo Kojima and to a way of telling stories that left a mark on an entire industry.

It is, above all, a filmmaking experiment generated with artificial intelligence. Every shot —the image concept, the animation and the voice— is born from generative AI tools, then edited together with narrative intent. We are interested in what happens when these tools are put at the service of a specific atmosphere: the tension of stealth, the loneliness of the soldier, the crunch of snow and the constant shadow of a nuclear threat.

The result aims to breathe the tone of the original work without copying it: intrigue, silence, decisions made in the dark and that moral weight that turned an infiltration mission into a reflection on war. An exercise in direction and editing where AI is the camera, and the tribute is the script.
